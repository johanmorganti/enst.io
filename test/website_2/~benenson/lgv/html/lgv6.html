Guidelines for language learning at the ENST

  • Obligations: credit system
  • Weekly courses by level
  • Students' syllabus in languages

    It is important for students to consider their three year language program as a whole spread over three years. The study of two foreign languages is obligatory.

    Given the importance of English for an engineer, all students take at least one English course during their whole degree program.

    One talks about second and third languages to designate language choices other than English. It is important for students to think in terms of an overall languages program especially when they want to study a second or third language.

    To refresh or to perfect a second language is always possible but beginning a new language, especially in second year, requires careful thought: the third year training period is often a long interruption. However, it is possible to reach a functional level in a language after one year of study. Experience shows that personal interest and motivation are the main criteria when choosing a second or a third language.

    How to plan one's language syllabus

    After evaluating your level in the different languages which interest you, it is necessary to plan your weekly schedule in blocks of 1 1/2, 3 or 4 1/2 hours (the last is very rare). In some exceptional cases, there some blocks of one hour placed during midday pauses.

    Weekly 1 1/2 hour classes are for students of intermediate level; for lower levels, students have 3 hour courses modules.

    Your courses may be reserved only to students of your graduating class or be mixed. Most often, it is a mix of both. This distinction is relevant for the department bulletin boards where class lists are posted.

    Starting from an upper-intermediate level, a student can follow either thematic courses or general courses.

    The choices of the student can be accepted only if they are realistic (scheduling between all the courses and the size of certain courses).

    The Language Department usually advizes not to take courses for two languages in which you are a beginner. Moreover, a student cannot begin a language in the third year because it is too difficult to acquire a functional level in only 6 months.

    The department gives advice to new students about their choices during level evaluation interviews at the beginning of their first year in school.

    Obligation of a level

    • In English Students must pass an in-house exam, or must have obtained a high enough score on one of the exterior exams: ARELS, Institut Britannique, TOEFL, Cambridge,...
    • In their Second Language Students can pass an exterior exam:
      • in German: Zertifikat Deutsch als Fremdsprache (ZDaF) or Zentrale Mittelstufenprüfung (ZMP) both certified by the Goethe Institut of München.
      • in Spanish: Diploma Basico of the School Ministery.
      • in Italian: European Certificate in Italian.
