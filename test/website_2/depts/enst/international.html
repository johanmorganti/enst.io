An International Perspective

Learning Languages: a Priority

One of the School's aims is to enable students to express themselves with ease and confidence in at least two foreign languages, one of them necessarily English. Whether it is to begin with a language or to perfect one's knowledge of a language already studied, a student can find a wide choice in the Language Department including English, German, Spanish, Arabic, Italian, Russian, Hebrew, Polish and Japanese.

Students can work on their oral expression in small groups. Magazines, newspapers, and audio and video tapes are available for students, who can also practice their skills in an English-language theater group and language tables in the School cafeteria.

International Connections

ENST is among one of the most internationally-oriented engineering schools: 22% of its students come from abroad. French students must spend at least two months overseas and three-quarters of the School's students carry out an internship in a non French-speaking country. There is also a strong involvement in the European Community's programs and a wide range of partnerships with more than fifty foreign universities.

Third Year Overseas

At the end of their program of study, students may spend an entire year overseas and receive their diploma from ENST and, in some cases, a degree from the university where they have spent the year. Opportunities include a joint diploma with the University of Stuttgart and Masters of Science in the United States.
