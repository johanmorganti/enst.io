The School and Industry

ENST is a gateway to the business world. Because of the school's close relationships with companies, students can prepare themselves for their future profession. The Department of Economics and Management provides the first contact with the corporate landscape. Instruction in economics not only touches upon the internal functioning of companies and their competitive and institutional environments. It includes cases studies, the strategic positioning of companies, and hands-on interviews in business settings.

Internships: a Major Stage in Learning about Industry

Between the first and second years, a worker-level internship of at least a month allows the future engineer to get an inside perspective on the production process and the daily reality of people who work in a company.

At the start of the third year, the engineering internship, carried out in conjunction with the second year Majors, allows the student to put into practice some of the knowledge gained at the School and especially to place him or her in the situation that will be found by a practicing engineer. At least two months of these internships must be done abroad.

The Alumni Relations Office

The Alumni association is a place of exchanges. Every 3 months, a "Télécom" magazine is published and the Association organizes meetings between students, alumni and business leaders. The association is responsible for the Alumni directory and helps students as well as experienced engineers to plan their careers.

Continuing Education

The programs for continuing education of ENST make it possible for engineers to advance in their careers and extend their areas of competence. Ninety different programs, built for designers and users of complex computing, electronic, and telecommunications systems, receive close to 2500 participants each year. Tailor made sessions are also organized for interested companies. A fast growing, the Continuing Education Department has made the School into one of the most important centers for the continuing training of engineers and managers in its areas of specialization. An annual catalogue of training sessions is published by the Continuing Education Department.

The Junior Enterprise: TELECOM Etudes

A genuine service company, TELECOM Etudes provides businesses with expertise at very competitive prices in a number of the disciplines taught at ENST.

Formacom: an Association for Training in Telecommunications

Students offer custom-designed courses for companies wishing to develop their expertise in the areas of information technologies and communications. Unique in its category, this association allows students to consolidate their knowledge while earning experience both in instruction and in business.
