Language Learning in Tandem

What is language learning in tandem?

Learning in tandem involves a form of open learning whereby people with different native languages work together in pairs

  • in order to learn each others' language(s)
  • to learn more about their partner and their partner's culture
  • to exchange additional - e.g. professional - knowledge.

Tandem principles

Tandem learning is learning in partnerships and autonomous intercultural learning.

There are 2 important prinicples:

  • Each partner has to benefit equally from working together, at least subjectively (Principle of Reciprocity). The same amount of time has to be spent on each language, both partners have to support each other equally, etc.
  • Each person is responsible for their own learning process and therefore establishes their own learning objectives and methods (Principle of Learner Autonomy). Objectives and methods are rarely the same for both partners.
    Each partner is available as an expert in their language and culture: they can read out loud, if desired, elaborate on a topic picked by the partner, correct mistakes, make suggestions for improvement, etc. However, they are usually not trained to teach: neither learning objectives, methods and evaluation can be expected of them, nor the systematic preparation of learning material (grammar, etc.).
    Tandem learning requires and promotes the ability to learn a language autonomously.

Language learning in tandem is easily adapted to a wide variety of language learning contexts: tandems between students with different native languages at a university, between exchange students and native students at a school, between English-speaking tourists in Spain and Spanish learners of English, between German teachers of French and French teachers of German – these are just a few examples. In tandem courses (usually intensive courses), the tandem work is combined with other course components.

Previous experience with tandem learning

  • in bi-cultural tandem intensive courses,
  • between learners with different native languages at the same university
  • via the Internet with partners in different countries

has proved extremely positive (see the language learning in tandem bibliography on this server).

Tandem tasks, along with other didactic aids supporting tandem work for learners and teachers, are published in this database.

Tandem partners generally sit across from each other and communicate orally (face-to-face tandem). Starting in 1992, however, the International E-Mail Tandem Network began to organise tandem à distance (in particular written tandem learning using e-mail and other Internet resources like MOOs) and to facilitate the development of new didactic aids for learners, foreign language teachers and network co-ordinators.

Tandem organisers

Language learning in tandem has been organised at many institutions in various countries. Information can be found, for example, on the following WWW servers:

  • The participating institutions actively involved in the expansion of the International E-Mail Tandem Network organise, in addition to tandem à distance, face-to-face tandem in tandem courses and in other contexts. The advising of tandem learners is increasingly taking place through individual counselling. The Tandem Server (Bochum, Dublin, Paris, Torino, Trier etc.), established by the network, provides for the publication of learning aids for learners as well as for teachers, tandem counsellors and organisers.
  • The Institute for German Language at the University of Freiburg/Fribourg (Switzerland) with UNI-TANDEM (one of the oldest university tandem agencies, which now also offers a guide to tandem learning).
  • The TANDEM® Network (TANDEM® Fundazioa), through which many private language schools have come together, one of which being TANDEM-Madrid, where the first tandem pairs outside of courses were created at the beginning of the 1980s.
