What is the International E-Mail Tandem Network?

In the International E-Mail-Tandem-Network, universities in many countries work together to enable their students to learn languages in tandem using e-mail. The network is now also open to all learners outside of universities.

The International E-Mail Tandem Network has been funded since July 1994 by the Commission of the European Union through projects in the LINGUA and ODL programmes. The network co-ordinator is Helmut Brammerts.

The network is made up of a consistently growing number of bilingual subnets, in which language learners of different native languages work together in order to help each other learn. Each subnet includes:

  • access to the central Tandem Agency,
  • at least one bilingual discussion forum, in which participants can discuss all kinds of topics with each other and can ask each other for advice in either language, as well as
  • a database, through which participants can access teaching and learning material and into which participants can enter documents themselves, such as background information about their own co untry or bilingual texts which have been written in conjunction with their tandem partner.

Two or more co-ordinators oversee each subnet: they help the participants, moderate discussion in the forum, make decisions concerning the content of the database, establish new forums and servic es, etc.

Development

Based on the positive experiences of the universities of Rhode Island (John Grandin, Norbert Hedderich) and Bochum (Helmut Brammerts) since the summer of 1992 with an English-German e-mail forum (RIBO-L) and the co-operation of their students using e-mail in tandem, the Ruhr-Universität Bochum began establishing the network in 1993, collaborating with the universities of Oviedo and Sheffield.

12 universities from now 10 European countries – since 1994, Aalborg (DK), Aarhus (DK), Bochum (D), Coimbra (P), Oviedo (E), ENST Paris (F), Sheffield (UK), Trier (D); since 1995, Dublin (IRL), Mitthögskolan (S), and HKL Sittard (NL); since 1996, Torino (I) – have been working together in the EU LINGUA project International E-Mail Tandem Network and in the EU Open and Distance Learning project Telematics for Autonomous and Intercultural Learning in Tandem in order to expand the network and to develop didactic materials designed for tandem partners, organisers, and foreign language teachers.

Other universities actively involved in the establishment of the network are Matsuyama (Japan), Rhode Island (USA) and Seoul (Korea).

How can you participate?

  • If you have been learning a foreign language for at least several months and would like to work in collaboration with a partner according to the tandem method of language learning, then write to the Tandem Agency:

    tandem@slf.ruhr-uni-bochum.de

    The message could be as follows: "My name is... I'm a professional / college student / high school student. My native language is English. I'm learning Dutch."
    You will find     help for tandem learning here in the tandem server.
    Please note that the summer months (June through August/September) are examination and holiday periods for most universites and schools in many countries. During this time each year it is very difficult to find tandem partners in other countries. - You can see how many tandem partners are presently available for each language by looking at the Tandem Agency statistics.

    Find the subnet in the list above which corresponds to your combination of native and target languages. On the corresponding Web pages for each subnet you will find further tips and explanations. There you will also find out how to subscribe to the forum through which you can get to know other participants who are learning in tandem with the same language combination as yourself.

  • If you are a foreign language instructor then do go ahead and let your students know about this opportunity to work with a tandem partner. It would be helpful if you were then able to counsel your students in this type of autonomous language learning.
    If you would like for your students or course participants to work in tandem with those of a particular colleague or at a particular institution in a foreign country, then you should notify the Tandem Agency and ask them to pair your students accordingly. Please then go ahead notify the co-ordinators of the appropriate subnet.
    Perhaps you would also like to participate in the forum     TANDEM-ORG, in which you can exchange ideas and information with organisers of tandem and foreign language instructors in various countries (contributions to the forum are usually in English).
  • If you wish to establish a new subnet, then please contact the network co-ordinator (see below).
